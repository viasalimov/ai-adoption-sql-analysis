# Key Findings

A summary of the main insights from the analysis. Figures come from the modeled 2022–2025 dataset (30 countries).

## Investment & priorities
- **AI spend as % of GDP reveals priorities better than absolute dollars.** Israel (3.50%) and Singapore (3.03%) lead, followed by Finland (2.45%) and Denmark (2.36%). These countries spend 7–15× more on AI relative to their economies than the bottom of the list — Nigeria (0.23%), Indonesia (0.30%), Mexico (0.31%).
- **The USA leads in absolute investment (~$385.5B) but ranks only 8th by share of GDP (1.68%)** because of the sheer size of its economy. Dollar volume ≠ national-level prioritization.

## Adoption growth
- **The USA had the fastest-growing AI user base** among the top 10, +7.57 percentage points per year (62.5% in 2022 → 85.2% in 2025), ahead of Israel (+6.80) and Denmark (+6.73). Finland closes the top 10 (+5.80; 49.2% → 66.6%).

## Risk
- **Deepfake-risk rankings within continents are mostly stable** 2022→2025 (China and the USA stay regional leaders). **Europe shifts the most**: Germany rose +2 (5th → 3rd) and Finland +1 (10th → 9th), while Ukraine, France, Denmark, Switzerland and Norway each slipped one place. Incident counts rose almost everywhere in absolute terms.

## Workforce
- **Reskilling return (wage premium ÷ reskilling reach) is highest in emerging markets.** India (1.75) and Kazakhstan (1.72) lead — high AI wage premiums despite modest reskilling coverage (India ~14%, Kazakhstan ~9%).
- **Developed markets show diminishing returns.** Germany (1.05), the UK (1.09) and Finland (1.10) have far wider reskilling coverage (30–32%) but a proportionally smaller premium — consistent with markets closer to saturation, where AI skills are less scarce.

## Note on NULLs
`generative_ai_share` is NULL for all 2022 rows, consistent with generative AI not being widely tracked before the dataset's later years. These were left as NULL for averages (so the mean reflects only real observations) rather than coerced to 0.
